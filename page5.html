🔐 How to Protect Yourself from Phishing:

  • Pause before clicking: Unexpected or emotional messages are designed to rush you — take a moment to think.
  • Verify the sender: Check the email address carefully. Scammers often impersonate trusted organizations with subtle typos.
  • Hover over links: Before you click, hover to preview the actual URL. If it looks suspicious or unrelated — don’t click.
  • Don’t download unknown files: Attachments with extensions like .exe, .zip, or .scr can be dangerous.
  • Watch for urgency or threats: “Act now” language is a red flag — legitimate institutions don’t use scare tactics.
  • Use MFA (multi-factor authentication): Even if your password is compromised, MFA adds a second line of defense.
